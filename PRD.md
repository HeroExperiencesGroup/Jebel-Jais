## 1. Purpose

Produce a premium, cinematic pitch deck used by Hero Experiences to present to H.H. Sheikh Ahmad of Ras Al Khaimah.

The deck makes two arguments simultaneously:
1. Hero is the right operating and activation partner to transform Jebel Jais into a world-class mountain destination.
2. Hero's ambition extends beyond Jebel Jais — to build a connected adventure ecosystem spanning the full diversity of Ras Al Khaimah (mountains, wadis, desert, coast).

The deck must feel worthy of the room it enters. Sheikh Ahmad's team will have seen many proposals. This one must stand apart visually and narratively from the moment the first slide appears.

This document is the **single source of truth** for copy, design direction, and HTML build. Claude Code must treat this PRD as the spec; all generated artifacts must conform to it.

## 2. Strategic positioning

### 2.1 The tagline

> One Emirate. Four Landscapes. Endless Adventure.

### 2.2 The thesis

Jebel Jais is already home to some of the region's most successful adventure attractions. The next chapter is not about adding another attraction — it is about transforming Jebel Jais into a complete mountain destination, and using it as the anchor of a connected adventure ecosystem that showcases the full diversity of Ras Al Khaimah.

Hero's ambition is not to manage attractions. It is to help Ras Al Khaimah build a tourism identity worthy of its extraordinary landscapes.

### 2.3 The defensive frame

This is not a management contract pitch. It is a vision for an entire emirate's tourism future — with Jebel Jais as the catalyst and Hero as the long-term strategic partner.

### 2.4 Target audience

- **Primary:** H.H. Sheikh Ahmad of Ras Al Khaimah — decision authority on Jebel Jais operating and development partnerships
- **Secondary:** RAK Tourism Development Authority leadership in the room
- **Tone they respond to:** visionary, restrained, respectful — never salesy; never overdesigned; credentials woven into the narrative, not paraded

### 2.5 What success looks like in the room

Sheikh Ahmad closes the meeting having visualised Jebel Jais not as a single mountain with ziplines on it, but as the anchor of an adventure destination ecosystem unlike anywhere else in the Gulf. The slide he remembers is Slide 10 — the RAK ecosystem map. It transforms the conversation from "operating Jebel Jais" to "building the future tourism identity of Ras Al Khaimah."

## 3. Design direction — Editorial Adventure Cinematic (locked v1)

### 3.1 The direction in one sentence

The deck looks like an unpublished National Geographic adventure feature on Ras Al Khaimah — cinematic landscape photography at scale, editorial typography, and the confident restraint of a brand that knows it has nothing to prove.

### 3.2 Reference points

- **National Geographic** — editorial cinematic photography; declarative headlines; the landscape is the argument
- **Patagonia** — adventure credibility without ostentation; human scale in large landscapes
- **Aman Resorts** — warm restraint; nothing unnecessary; confidence through subtraction
- **Land Rover** — capability implied, never shouted; terrain as character
- **Apple keynote** — one idea per slide; silence is not wasted space

### 3.3 Visual feel

- Cinematic photography: golden hour, mountain mist, human figures dwarfed by landscape
- Large panoramic imagery — the photograph does the persuading
- Minimal text — a headline and a few lines, never a paragraph where a sentence will do
- Warm natural colours — drawn from RAK's terrain, not from a brand palette
- Premium but not luxury — adventure, exploration, discovery
- Human scale — people experiencing places, not empty landscapes or stock smiles
- No stock tourism photography; no corporate imagery; no flowcharts as primary content

### 3.4 What this is NOT

- Not a Dubai luxury brochure (no gold-on-black, no skyscraper backgrounds)
- Not corporate slideware (no rounded-corner cards, no bar charts, no icon sets)
- Not a tourism government report (no dense tables, no flowcharts as slide content)
- Not Canva defaults (no illustrated icons, no badge-style awards treatment)
- Not a standard adventure-tourism pitch (no neon, no GoPro-style action shots)

### 3.5 Color system

| Token | Value | Role |
|---|---|---|
| `--bone` | `#F2EBD9` | Primary background on restrained slides |
| `--ink` | `#1A1410` | Primary text; near-black warm |
| `--stone` | `#857260` | Section dividers, fine rules, meta labels |
| `--terra` | `#7A3E22` | Warm accent — pull-quote hairlines, rare emphasis |
| `--mountain` | `#252E2A` | Dark surface for immersive slides |
| `--gold` | `#C49540` | Map nodes, ecosystem connectors, Slide 10 only |
| `--sky` | `#4A7898` | Coastal/water elements, used very sparingly |

### 3.6 Typography

Inherits the `partner-decks` shared design system:

| Role | Font | Fallback |
|---|---|---|
| Display / headlines | Playfair Display | Georgia, Times New Roman, serif |
| Editorial italic | Newsreader | Georgia, Times New Roman, serif |
| Body / sans | Inter | system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif |
| Meta / small caps | Inter | system-ui, sans-serif |

### 3.7 The six rules

1. **Photography carries the argument.** Full-bleed on immersive slides. The image earns its space or it doesn't appear.
2. **Typography is editorial.** Sentence case. Short. Declarative. Never persuasive adjectives.
3. **Colour comes from place.** Warm stone, mountain shadow, coastal teal — drawn from RAK's terrain, not from a branding palette.
4. **Rhythm alternates.** Immersive slides carry atmosphere; restrained slides carry information. The contrast is the pacing.
5. **Zero decoration.** No icons, no gradients as decoration, no drop shadows, no rounded corners as style. Every element carries information or atmosphere.
6. **One idea per slide.** If a slide is trying to say two things, it is two slides.

### 3.8 Slide 10 — special direction

Slide 10 (The Adventure Ecosystem) is the visual centrepiece of the deck. It requires a bespoke illustrated map of Ras Al Khaimah — not a screenshot of Google Maps, not a stock tourism graphic.

The map shows five experience zones connected by a single visual narrative radiating from Jebel Jais:
- **Jebel Jais** — the anchor at the centre
- **Wadi Showka** — nature and outdoor recreation
- **Defender Experience Centre** — off-road adventure
- **Desert Experiences** — heritage and exploration
- **Coastal Experiences** — marine adventure and discovery

The map must be beautiful enough to be remembered. It is the moment the conversation shifts from "who manages Jebel Jais" to "who builds the adventure future of Ras Al Khaimah."

Visual direction: dark, atmospheric, cinematic. The territory rendered in warm earth tones. The five nodes glow in warm gold. Connection paths are elegant dashed lines — not data-visualisation arrows. The whole slide should feel like a premium expedition atlas.

## 5. Content rules

### 5.1 Voice

- **Declarative, not persuasive.** "Hero operates award-winning heritage experiences across the UAE." NOT "Hero is proud to be one of the leading…"
- **Short sentences.** Most under 12 words.
- **No marketing adjectives:** world-class, breathtaking, unforgettable, bespoke, curated, elevated, unparalleled, seamless, holistic, synergy
- **No exclamation points. Ever.**
- **British English:** recognised, organisation, programme, centre, travelling
- **Numerals over words** for any figure ≥ 10

### 5.2 Headlines

- Sentence case (not Title Case, not ALL CAPS) — except cover labels and section openers in small caps
- Maximum 8 words per headline
- One headline per slide

### 5.3 Copy source

The master copy for all 16 slides is provided in the project brief (June 2026). Claude Code must treat that copy as the approved baseline. Changes to copy require explicit instruction.

### 5.4 Banned in all generated content

- Marketing adjectives listed in §5.1
- Exclamation points
- Title Case headlines
- Stock icon sets, badge-style award marks, rounded-corner cards, gradients as decoration
- Dense bullet-point lists (no more than 6 items per slide; never multi-column bullet dumps)
- The word "journey" used as a noun (e.g. "embark on a journey" — banned)

## 7. Deliverables

### 7.1 Phase B0 — Per-slide descriptive briefs

Before HTML is written, every slide gets a descriptive brief under `/jebel-jais/briefs/` covering: copy + structure, image direction, atmosphere note. One Markdown file per slide.

### 7.2 Phase B — HTML build (production, hosted)

The HTML deck is the canonical deliverable. Hosted at `jebel-jais.hero-experience.com` (or equivalent agreed subdomain) via Cloudflare DNS → GitHub Pages.

**Technical spec:**
- Static site, no backend, no CMS
- Vanilla HTML and CSS; minimal vanilla JavaScript only where needed
- Inherits `/shared/` design system from `partner-decks` repo where applicable — new tokens defined in `/jebel-jais/css/tokens.css`
- Single scrolling editorial document with keyboard navigation
- **Mobile-first** — phone first, then tablet, then 16:9 laptop
- Self-hosted webfonts (same fonts as `partner-decks/shared/fonts/`)
- WebP imagery with JPG fallback; responsive `srcset`; lazy loading below the fold
- Target page weight: cover under 2MB; total deck under 15MB
- Print stylesheet: `Cmd+P → Save as PDF` produces clean PDF

### 7.3 PDF leave-behind (derived)

Generated from HTML via browser print-to-PDF. Print stylesheet locks layouts to A4 landscape. File name: `jebel-jais-hero-experiences-YYYY-MM.pdf`

### 7.4 Out of scope

- Video and motion
- Multi-language versions
- CMS-driven content updates
